Effective 5/5/2021
53B-27-501.  Free expression policies.
(1) As used in this section, "free expression policy" means an institution's policy, regulation, or other expectation related to student expression.
(2) An institution shall:
(a) publish the institution's free expression policies:
(i) in the institution's student handbook; and
(ii) on the institution's website;
(b) include information about the institution's free expression policies in an orientation program for students enrolled in the institution; and
(c) develop a program, procedures, and materials to ensure that an individual who has responsibility for the discipline or education of a student at the institution understands the institution's free expression policies.
(3) An individual described in Subsection (2)(c) includes an institution:
(a) administrator;
(b) campus police officer;
(c) residence life official; and
(d) faculty member.
(4) An institution shall ensure that a free expression policy is consistent with the provisions of this chapter.
